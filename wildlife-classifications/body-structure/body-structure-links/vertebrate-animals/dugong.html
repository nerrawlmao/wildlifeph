Dugong

Dugong dugon

The dugong is a marine mammal. It is one of four living species of the order Sirenia, which also includes three species of manatees. It is the only living representative of the once-diverse family Dugongidae.

Appearance

The dugong (Dugong dugon) has a distinctive and rather endearing appearance, well-suited to its aquatic, herbivorous lifestyle. Often described as having a "chubby" or "fusiform" (spindle-shaped) body.

Habitat & Ecology

    Biome Marine Habitat Type Marine Neritic, Marine Intertidal Climate Zone Tropical, Subtropical

Dugongs are generally found in warm coastal waters with large numbers concentrated in wide and shallow protected bays. The dugong is the only strictly marine herbivorous mammal, as all species of manatee utilise fresh water to some degree.

Dugong mating habits are fascinating but somewhat challenging to observe in detail due to their aquatic and shy nature. They are Polyandrous, have a mating trains to compete for a female dugong in which they get aggressive and lastly lekking behavior.

Conservation Status

Population Trend Decreasing
IUCN Red List Vulnerable (VU)

It is considered as a Vulnerable Species due to decreasing amounts of population.

Population Threats

The population is believed to be decreasing due to habitat lost and degradation, incidental capture, hunting and poaching, boat collisions, climate change, noise pollution, and low reproduction rate

Population Number

The global population trend for dugongs is decreasing, and they are classified as Vulnerable on the IUCN Red List, with some regional populations (like those in East Africa and China) being critically endangered or functionally extinct.

Ecological Niche

The ecological niche of the dugong (Dugong dugon) is that of a large, specialized, herbivorous marine mammal that acts as a vital "gardener" of shallow, tropical and subtropical coastal ecosystems, primarily through its grazing on seagrass meadows.
