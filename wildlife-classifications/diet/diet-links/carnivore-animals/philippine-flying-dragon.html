Philippine Flying Dragon

Draco spilopterus

The Philippine Flying Dragon is a small, arboreal lizard renowned for its gliding ability, facilitated by wing-like membranes called patagia. Endemic to the Philippines, it thrives in forested regions, using its gliding prowess to navigate the canopy and evade predators.

Appearance

Draco spilopterus possesses a slender, elongated body with specialized ribs that support its patagia, enabling gliding between trees. Its coloration typically includes shades of brown and gray, providing camouflage within its forest habitat. Males feature a prominent yellow dewlap used for territorial and mating displays.

Habitat & Ecology

    Biome Terrestrial Habitat Type Tropical forests, including secondary and disturbed forests Climate Zone Tropical

This species is found in the Philippines, inhabiting densely wooded areas where it can glide between trees. It prefers forested environments, including secondary and disturbed forests, and is known to forage in the canopy.

Population Threats

While not currently endangered, potential threats include habitat loss due to deforestation and human encroachment. However, the species' adaptability to secondary forests helps mitigate these risks.

Exact population numbers are not documented, but the species is described as abundant within its habitat range.

Ecological Niche

As an insectivore, the Philippine Flying Dragon plays a role in controlling insect populations, particularly ants and termites, contributing to the ecological balance within its forest ecosystem.
