Palawan Binturong

Arctictis binturong whitei

The Palawan Binturong, also known as the Palawan bearcat, is a subspecies of the binturong that is endemic to the island of Palawan in the Philippines.

Population Size

UNKNOWN

Weight

15-25 KG

Length

4.6 FT

Appearance

The Palawan Binturong is a medium-sized mammal with a long (up to 1.4 meters), stocky body covered in coarse, thick black-brown fur. Distinctive features include ears lined with white fur and long, white whiskers that can be as long as its head. They also have a strong prehensile tail, which they use like an extra limb for climbing and hanging.

Habitat & Ecology

    Biome Terrestrial Habitat Type Forest, Artificial/Terrestrial Climate Zone Tropical

Palawan Binturongs inhabit tropical rainforests on the island of Palawan. They can also be found in agricultural areas and near human settlements.

Female Binturongs are one of the few mammals capable of delayed implantation (also known as embryonic diapause). This means that after fertilization, the embryo doesn't immediately implant in the uterus. It can remain in a state of suspended development for a variable period before implanting and continuing to develop into a fetus.

Conservation Status

Population Trend Decreasing

It is considered as a Vulnerable Species due to decreasing amounts of population.

Population Threats

The Palawan Binturongs is vulnerable, primarily due to habitat loss and deforestations. This deforestation is driven by logging, mining, agricultural expansion, and urbanization. They are also hunted for food and trade in which people also make some traditional medicine resulting in it's decrease in population.

Population Number

There is no precise, current estimate for the total population size of the Palawan binturong due to it's elusive nature, dense habitat and also limited research.

Ecological Niche

The ecological niche of the Palawan Binturong is that of a medium-sized, arboreal (tree-dwelling) omnivore playing several important roles within the tropical rainforest ecosystem of Palawan. It is a Seed Disperser, a Small Predator/Prey and a Scavenger
