Rufous Hornbill

Buceros hydrocorax

The Rufous Hornbill is a striking bird species endemic to the Philippines, known for its vibrant reddish-brown plumage and large, curved bill.

Common Name

Rufous Hornbill

Scientific Name

Buceros hydrocorax

Size

60–65 cm

Weight

Males 1.5–2 kg; females slightly lighter

Appearance

The Rufous Hornbill is characterized by its predominantly black body and rufous (reddish-brown) head and neck. It possesses a large, curved red bill with a prominent casque, which is more developed in males. The tail feathers are white, providing a striking contrast to its dark plumage.

Taxonomy

Kingdom Animalia
Phylum Chordata
Class Aves
Order Bucerotiformes
Family Bucerotidae
Genus Buceros
Species Buceros hydrocorax

Habitat & Ecology

Geographic Range Endemic to the Philippines
Habitat Primary and secondary lowland rainforests, montane forests up to 2,100 meters elevation
Specific Locations Northern subspecies: Luzon and Marinduque
Southern subspecies: Mindanao, Dinagat, Siargao, Balut, Bucas, Talicud, Basilan

The Rufous Hornbill is endemic to the Philippines and is found in various forest habitats across different islands.

Habits & Lifestyle

Diet Primarily frugivorous; also eats insects, small reptiles, and small mammals
Foraging Behavior Typically forages in the canopy of large trees; occasionally descends to the ground
Social Structure Often seen in pairs or small family groups; strong family bonds
Vocalization Loud, resonant "tok-tok-tok" calls, audible up to 1.5 km away

Mating Habits

Breeding Season March to June
Nesting Nests in natural cavities of large trees; female seals herself inside
Incubation Period Approximately 37–46 days
Female Confinement Female remains sealed in the nest for up to 4–5 months
Lifespan Estimated between 35–50 years in the wild

Conservation Status

IUCN Status Vulnerable (VU)
CITES Listing Appendix II
Philippine Law Protected under Republic Act No. 9147

The Rufous Hornbill faces threats such as habitat destruction and hunting, leading to its Vulnerable status.

Ecological Niche

Rufous Hornbills play a vital ecological role in forest ecosystems. As frugivores, they contribute to seed dispersal, aiding in forest regeneration and maintaining biodiversity. Additionally, by consuming insects and small animals, they help regulate pest populations.

Population Threats

The Rufous Hornbill faces several threats, primarily habitat destruction due to logging, agriculture, and infrastructure development. Hunting and poaching for the illegal pet trade and traditional uses also contribute to their decline. Climate change poses an additional threat by affecting habitat and food availability.
