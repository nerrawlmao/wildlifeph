Philippine Naked-backed Fruit Bat

Dobsonia chapmani

The Philippine Naked-backed Fruit Bat is a large, fruit-eating megabat native to the Philippines, easily recognized by its distinct "naked" back where wing membranes attach directly to the spine. Once thought extinct, it was rediscovered in the 21st century, but it remains Critically Endangered due to extensive habitat loss and hunting.

Appearance

This bat has olive-brown to dark gray fur and large eyes suited for night vision. Its most distinctive trait is the absence of fur along the back, where the wing membranes fuse with the midline of its back—giving the "naked-backed" look. Its ears are rounded and prominent, while its body is streamlined for agile flight.

Habitat & Ecology

Endemic to the Philippine islands of Cebu and Negros, it roosts in caves and forages in surrounding forests for fruit. It requires intact forest corridors for survival, which have been heavily fragmented due to human activity.

Conservation Status

Population Trend Decreasing
IUCN Red List Critically Endangered (CR)

This species was feared extinct until its rediscovery in the 21st century. It is now one of the rarest bats in the world. Deforestation and cave disturbance are its biggest threats. There are conservation efforts including habitat restoration and protection in areas like the Central Cebu Protected Landscape.

Population Threats

The biggest threats are habitat loss due to logging, mining, and agricultural conversion, as well as hunting for food. Roosting caves are often disturbed or destroyed. Forest fragmentation limits the bat's ability to find food and safe roosting areas. Without forest corridors, this species is at severe risk of extinction.

Population Number

Exact numbers are unknown, but the species is extremely rare. Surveys have confirmed a few small colonies in forested parts of Cebu and Negros, totaling no more than a few hundred individuals. Conservationists continue to monitor these populations closely.

Ecological Niche

As a frugivore, the Philippine Naked-backed Fruit Bat is a key seed disperser. It helps maintain forest health by spreading seeds of native trees, especially those that depend on bats for regeneration. Its extinction would negatively impact forest biodiversity and structure.
