Philippine Flat Headed Frog

Barbourula kalimantanensis

The Philippine flat-headed frog (Barbourula kalimantanensis) is a rare species of frog native to the Philippines. It is known for its distinctive flattened head and fully aquatic lifestyle, often found in clean, fast-flowing mountain streams. Notably, this frog is one of the few known species to lack lungs, breathing entirely through its skin.

Appearance

This frog has a notably flattened head and body, with smooth, dark greenish to brown skin that helps it blend with the stream bed. Its limbs are strong and adapted for aquatic life, and it lacks lungs, relying on cutaneous respiration.

The Philippine flat-headed frog inhabits clean, cool, fast-flowing mountain streams in forested areas. Its aquatic adaptations and lunglessness make it especially sensitive to water quality and habitat disturbance.

Population Threats

The primary threats include water pollution, habitat destruction from deforestation and mining, and climate change affecting stream environments.

Exact population numbers are unknown due to the frog's elusive nature and difficult habitat.

As a fully aquatic predator, the Philippine flat-headed frog helps control aquatic insect populations and contributes to freshwater ecosystem health.
