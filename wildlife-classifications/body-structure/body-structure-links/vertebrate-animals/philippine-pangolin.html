Philippine Pangolin

Manis culionensis

The Philippine Pangolin, also known as the Palawan Pangolin or "balintong," is a medium-sized, scale-covered mammal endemic to the Palawan province of the Philippines. Distinguished by its smaller scales and shorter head-to-body ratio compared to its relatives, this elusive creature plays a crucial role in controlling insect populations.

Appearance

The Philippine Pangolin is covered in overlapping brownish to creamy yellow scales made of keratin, providing armor-like protection. It has a small, conical head with a long, sticky tongue used for feeding. Unlike other pangolins, it has a shorter head-to-body ratio and smaller scales, setting it apart from its close relatives.

Habitat & Ecology

    Biome Terrestrial Habitat Type Primary and secondary lowland forests, grasslands, agricultural lands, and forest-scrub mosaics Climate Zone Tropical

Endemic to the Palawan faunal region, including islands such as Palawan, Busuanga, Culion, and Calauit. Prefers habitats rich in ants and termites, often found in areas with fig trees, which provide both food and shelter.

Mating behaviors are not well-documented, but similar to other pangolins, they are likely solitary except during mating. Females give birth to a single offspring, which is carried on the mother's tail or back.

Conservation Status

Population Trend Decreasing
IUCN Red List Critically Endangered (CR)

The species faces severe threats from illegal wildlife trade and habitat destruction. Despite legal protections, enforcement is challenging, and the demand for pangolin scales and meat continues to drive poaching. Conservation efforts focus on habitat protection and anti-poaching measures.

Population Threats

The primary threats to the Philippine Pangolin include illegal hunting for its scales and meat, driven by high demand in traditional medicine markets. Habitat loss due to deforestation and land conversion for agriculture further exacerbates the decline. The species' elusive nature makes population monitoring difficult, hindering conservation efforts.

Population Number

Exact population numbers are unknown due to the species' secretive behavior and limited research. However, anecdotal reports and confiscation records indicate a significant decline in recent years. Continued habitat degradation and poaching suggest that the population is decreasing.

Ecological Niche

As a specialized insectivore, the Philippine Pangolin plays a vital role in controlling ant and termite populations, contributing to the balance of its ecosystem. Its foraging behavior aids in soil aeration, and its burrows provide shelter for other species, highlighting its ecological importance.
