Philippine Warty Pig

Sus philippensis

The Philippine Warty Pig is a wild pig species endemic to the Philippines, particularly found on Luzon and surrounding islands. Recognized by its distinctive facial warts and mane-like crest, this species plays a crucial role in forest ecosystems by aiding in seed dispersal and soil aeration.

Appearance

The Philippine Warty Pig has a dark gray to black bristly coat, with a prominent mane running from the head down the back, especially noticeable in males. Males possess two pairs of facial warts and larger tusks compared to females. Both sexes have a tuft of hair on the crown and white or yellowish tufts on the lower jaw.
